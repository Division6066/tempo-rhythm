## One-line positioning

Tempo Flow is an overwhelm-first personal operating system for neurodivergent people.

## Who Tempo is *for*

- People with ADHD, dyslexia, autism, or chronic-overwhelm patterns, who have been failed by traditional productivity apps.
- Their reality is: motivation is cyclical, executive function is spiky, shame spirals are real, and "just get organized" is the enemy.

## What Tempo is *against*

- Streak-break shaming ("you missed 3 days").
- Gamification that punishes absence.
- Silent AI changes that erode trust.
- Productivity nihilism (empty states that imply "you haven't done anything").
- Walls of configuration. Calendar apps that require you to learn them.
- Locking features behind payment when the user is in crisis mode.

## What Tempo stands *for*

- Welcoming the user back after a gap, every single time.
- Making undo the default reflex, not the exception.
- Coaching as companionship, not surveillance.
- Accountability on a dial, not an absolute.
- Personalization by AI at the render layer, never by cluttered schema.

## Tone markers

Short sentences. Warm, not saccharine. Assumes the user is competent and tired. Never corporate. Never patronizing. Humor is gentle and sparse. Medical and therapy language is earned, not performed.

## Visual mood

"Soft Editorial" — magazine-like typography, generous whitespace, muted saturated accents, rounded corners without being juvenile, serif headings (Newsreader), sans body (Inter), mono accents (IBM Plex Mono).
